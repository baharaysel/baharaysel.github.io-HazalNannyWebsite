About me

Mrs Asik possesses the most convenient type of educational background, qualifications, professional career and personal character for the business she intends to start-up in the UK. She has been operating in the education sector for many years and she has an excellent endowment of being able to communicate with young people and children in a marvellous manner. As a counsellor teacher, she has taken care of many young students during her career at various institutions throughout years and she has contributed to the well-being of many students and other children in need in a way that has enhanced their learning processes and knowledge.

Mrs Asik has graduated from the department of Philosophy at Anadolu University, Eskisehir, Turkey in 2015. During her bachelor’s degree studies, she improved her knowledge and skills on many areas and forged for a unique set of qualifications throughout the years. She took very efficient steps and attended many training programs during her studies to develop a successful career in the field of education of young people and children in need.

Between 09.2014 and 06.2015, she took Pedagogical Training Program at her own university, Anadolu University and gained the Paediatric Certificate. She then successfully completed occupational trainings on Teaching Practice, Special Teaching Methods, Instructional Technology and Material Design, Educational Psychology, and Educational Science.

In the Spring of 2017, she began teaching as a philosophy teacher at Sinav Education College at Ankara, Turkey, where she attended Counsellor Workshop and completed her training which granted her the title of counsellor teacher as well. While working there for one academic year, she also attended another workshop and completed her Guidance Certificate Program in 2018. Later on, she completed Participation in Education Program at Istanbul Gelisim University at Ankara, Turkey, whereby she got training on the topic of Communication Disorder with Children and Teenagers.

Having been interested in care for students and children with special needs, she decided to improve her knowledge on this field further and enrolled in Turkish Sign Language Training course in Ankara in 2019. Beginning with the Summer Term of 2019, she started to work at Toprak Private Education Course in Ankara, Turkey and since then, she has been teaching as a counsellor and philosophy teacher there.

Her experience as a counsellor teacher has so far turned out to be what she has desired to have for her career. During the time being, she has had the chance to take care and deal with so many young students and children in need and spared a substantial amount of time for them with a unique sense of self-devotion. Dedicated to breathing the same environment with children and 3 young students all the time, she has found the happiness and tranquillity in being with them and making them happy. She pretty much enjoyed spending her time with them and has always been the favourite teacher of theirs as a sincere and cordial counsellor educator.

She invested a considerable portion of her time to specialize on the students and young children with special needs and mastered her skills accordingly via different training programs and workshops. While developing a career in the education sector as a philosophy teacher, she also chose a path to become a counsellor teacher where she managed to take care of children and young students more closely and deal with their problems and issues in a more convenient and instructive way. Besides, she gave private tutoring lessons to a lot of students and children with special needs such as children with dyslexia and autism and other disabilities. She contributed to the well-being and the learning processes of the children effectively and she became a high in demand teacher in the eyes of many families in need those kinds of services.

Projects for kids

Maked a bug hotel

Are your little ones fascinated by creepy crawlies? Bring A Bug's Life to your back garden with these fabulous bug hotels! Not only are you creating an educational project for your kids, but helping out mother nature too.

Make your own kites

These fun DIY easy crafts for kids will double up as a great outdoor activity once you've finished!

Make salt dough ornaments

Want to start your next art project but worried you don't have any craft supplies in the house? We've got the perfect activity for you! All you need to make your own salt dough is flour, salt and water. You can even decorate your creations with food colouring, paints or pens. If you don't have these supplies the kids will still love mixing and rolling the dough...

Paint a plant pot and plant seeds in it

This lovely project combines two fun activities, painting and planting. Grab your paints, a terracotta plant pot and get cracking on this art project! There isn't much more to it. If you want the painting to last though, add a layer of waterproof varnish once the paint is dry. Once finished, plant your little one's favourite flower!

Discover easy origami

One of the simplest ideas in this round-up to make as all you need is some paper to get going. If you have older children who want a challenge try this How to make an Origami Lion. For younger children, you can't beat the fun of a simple origami hat.

Junk model a marble maze

Marbles are as popular now as they ever were! Why not upcycle some of your old cardboard and create a fun marble puzzle for the little ones. Cardboard Labrinth Game is great fun for slightly older kids. You can adjust your maze to suit the age of the child too. Small children might want to roll the marbles around using loo rolls. Remember always to supervise when playing with marbles.

Make your own mosaic pictures

Children are fascinated by colour, and this simple activity can be easily set up without needing any special supplies. Simply grab some coloured paper, scissors and PVA glue. You could also use old magazines and cut them up into lots of different pieces (the kids can help with that bit too), then use them to build up mosaic art!

Make playdough dinosaur fossils craft ideas for kids

This simple arts and crafts idea which sparks a chat about your favourite dinosaurs. Grab a tub of play dough, a couple of dinosaur toys and make your own fossils. Don't have any dinosaur toys? See what other household items you can push into the clay to look like dinosaur claws! Or adapt to suit your child's favourite toys - My Little Pony fossils anyone?
